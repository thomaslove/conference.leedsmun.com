The Topics

The question of the prevention of autonomous weapons

The question of autonomous weapons remains, so far, relatively unanswered. The rise in the use of armed and unarmed drones has led to numerous discussions and arguments about their usage, however, the majority of these have focused around human remote-controlled UAVs. Programmed autonomous drones, or indeed in the future land based weaponised rovers, raise numerous questions of accountability, ethics and control. In a growing digital age, where wars are increasingly fought from control rooms rather than on the ground, autonomous weapons could be the next step in the journey. Topics focused around areas where technology is still very new or is a future possibility, have been debated and agreed upon by the UN before, and have provided us with some iconic resolutions and conventions. This topic, therefore, we hope will provide a invigorating topic for the LeedsMUN DISEC Committee.

The Question of Disputes in the South China Sea

The South China Sea has been a heavily disputed body of water for centuries; with China, Singapore, Taiwan, Vietnam, Cambodia, the Philippines and Brunei all holding territorial claims. In recent years the military and naval presence in the sea has escalated, leading to an increasingly volatile situation. China’s building of an oil rig and artificial islands have been widely criticised by the international community. The USA have answered China’s claims of total sovereignty by sending warships, and other states in the area have appealed to the UN to diffuse the situation. All of these factors contribute to make the issue a challenging topic for the DISEC committee to consider
