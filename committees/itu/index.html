The Topics

The Question of Global Internet Accessibility

Here in the developed world we tend to take Internet access for granted. However, over half of the world’s population does not have access to the Internet. Unequal access to the Internet is a major problem across the world, and is arguably a barrier to development and education for several millions of people. We aim to solve the problem of Internet provision in LEDCs, and in turn improve access to the Internet for millions of people worldwide.

Regulating the Dark Web

The Internet is, arguably, one of the most significant inventions of the past 30 years. Its influence and meteoric expansion have completely changed the face of human society in many ways. An increasing number sites exist on hidden and encrypted networks such as the Dark Web including online illegal drug marketplaces such as the Silk Road. Th e aims of this debate are to establish a framework for using the Dark Web, and what can be done in order to achieve agreed upon goals of enforcing the law on the Dark Web.
